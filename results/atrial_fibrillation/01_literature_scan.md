# Literature Scan: Atrial Fibrillation

## Search Strategy

Three-pass PubMed search strategy covering 8 thematic domains with targeted
follow-up searches for top candidate questions.

**Pass 1 — Broad landscape (8 searches):**
1. DOAC comparisons (apixaban vs rivaroxaban) — 62 results
2. Rhythm vs rate control — 445 results
3. Catheter ablation vs drug therapy — 574 results
4. Anticoagulation in CKD/liver disease — 264 results
5. Early rhythm control — 52 results
6. LAA closure vs anticoagulation — 353 results
7. AF in heart failure subgroups — 176 results
8. Target trial emulation in AF — 13 results

**Pass 2 — Targeted per-question searches (5 searches):**
1. Apixaban vs rivaroxaban in CKD — 191 results
2. DOACs in cirrhosis (rate-limited, supplemented by Pass 1)
3. Early rhythm control + HF subgroups — 29 results
4. Catheter ablation in HFpEF — 68 results
5. LAAC in CKD/high bleeding risk — 320 results

**Pass 3 — Citation chaining and TTE methodology verification:**
- Forward citation search for Fu 2024 CKD study
- Dedicated TTE + anticoagulant + AF search (7 results)
- WebSearch for TTE methodology in AF (confirmed 7+ TTE studies in AF)

## 1. DOAC Comparative Effectiveness

### Landmark RCTs
The pivotal DOAC trials (RE-LY, ROCKET-AF, ARISTOTLE, ENGAGE AF-TIMI 48)
established individual DOACs vs warfarin but did not include head-to-head
DOAC comparisons. No completed RCT directly compares apixaban to rivaroxaban.

### Key Observational Studies

| PMID | Authors | Year | Design | Key Finding |
|------|---------|------|--------|-------------|
| 36315950 | Lau et al. | 2022 | Multinational cohort (6 countries) | Apixaban associated with lower bleeding than rivaroxaban; similar stroke prevention |
| 34932078 | Ray et al. | 2021 | US cohort (Medicare/Medicaid) | Rivaroxaban associated with higher major ischemic/hemorrhagic events vs apixaban |
| 39442621 | Dawwas & Cuker | 2025 | US population-based | Rivaroxaban vs apixaban: higher stroke (HR 1.23) and bleeding (HR 1.60) |
| 38878942 | Shurrab et al. | 2024 | Canadian population-based | Apixaban superior to rivaroxaban in older patients |
| 33544915 | Perreault et al. | 2021 | Canadian PS-matched cohort | High-dose rivaroxaban vs apixaban: rivaroxaban had higher bleeding |
| 36253039 | Jaksa et al. | 2022 | UK primary care | Comparable effectiveness, apixaban safer profile |
| 38511316 | Chiv et al. | 2024 | Population-based cohort | Edoxaban vs apixaban in elderly: comparable effectiveness |

### Target Trial Emulation in DOAC Comparisons
| PMID | Authors | Year | Design | Key Finding |
|------|---------|------|--------|-------------|
| 34271110 | Mei et al. | 2021 | TTE, Medicare | Dabigatran vs rivaroxaban: dabigatran superior for composite endpoint |

### Additional TTE Evidence
| PMID | Authors | Year | Design | Key Finding |
|------|---------|------|--------|-------------|
| 36252244 | (Annals of Internal Medicine) | 2022 | **TTE**, PS-matched (N=19,894) | Apixaban vs rivaroxaban in AF + VHD: apixaban lower stroke/SE (HR 0.57) and lower bleeding (HR 0.51) |

**Gap:** One TTE study (PMID 36252244) has compared apixaban vs rivaroxaban in AF
patients with valvular heart disease, and Mei 2021 compared dabigatran vs rivaroxaban
in a general Medicare population. However, **no TTE has compared apixaban vs
rivaroxaban in the advanced CKD population** — the subgroup with the greatest clinical
uncertainty given underpowered RCTs and altered pharmacokinetics.

## 2. Rhythm Control vs Rate Control

### Landmark RCTs

| PMID | Trial | Year | Design | Key Finding |
|------|-------|------|--------|-------------|
| 12466506 | AFFIRM | 2002 | RCT, N=4,060 | No mortality benefit of rhythm vs rate control |
| 32865375 | EAST-AFNET 4 | 2020 | RCT, N=2,789 | Early rhythm control reduced CV death/stroke/HF hospitalization |
| 34447995 | EAST-AFNET 4 (substudy) | 2022 | RCT subanalysis | Benefit in symptomatic and asymptomatic patients |
| 33351042 | RATE-AF | 2020 | RCT, N=160 | Digoxin vs bisoprolol for rate control: similar QoL |
| 37433659 | Koldenhof et al. | 2023 | RCT | CCB vs beta-blocker for rate control |

### Observational and TTE Studies

| PMID | Authors | Year | Design | Key Finding |
|------|---------|------|--------|-------------|
| 36063552 | Kim et al. | 2022 | Korean nationwide PS-weighted | Early rhythm control beneficial even in low-risk patients |
| 35621202 | Dickow et al. | 2022 | US cohort | EAST-AFNET 4 findings generalizable to US population |
| 35589174 | Kim et al. | 2022 | Korean nationwide | Early rhythm control benefits across age groups |
| 41121356 | Liu et al. | 2025 | **TTE** | Digoxin vs beta-blocker in AF+HF: digoxin associated with **higher** mortality (RR 1.21) and HF hospitalization |
| 41342526 | Kriz et al. | 2025 | CYCLE cohort | Early rhythm control in acute decompensated HF |
| 40057866 | Kang et al. | 2025 | Nationwide cohort | Early rhythm control in CKD patients: beneficial |

**Gap:** Despite multiple observational replications of EAST-AFNET 4, no TTE study
has examined early rhythm control in the specific HF subpopulations (HFpEF vs
HFrEF). Liu 2025 applied TTE to rate-control drug choice (digoxin vs BB) in AF+HF,
but rhythm vs rate control in HF subgroups remains un-emulated.

## 3. Catheter Ablation vs Drug Therapy

### Landmark RCTs

| PMID | Trial | Year | Design | Key Finding |
|------|-------|------|--------|-------------|
| 30874766 | CABANA | 2019 | RCT, N=2,204 | ITT: no significant difference in primary composite; per-protocol: ablation superior |
| 29385358 | CASTLE-AF | 2018 | RCT, N=363 | Ablation reduced death/HF hospitalization in HFrEF |
| 33197159 | EARLY-AF | 2021 | RCT, N=303 | First-line cryoablation superior to drugs for AF recurrence |
| 36342178 | Andrade et al. | 2023 | RCT (EARLY-AF extension) | Ablation reduced AF progression at 3 years |
| 40602939 | PRAGUE-25 | 2025 | RCT | Ablation vs lifestyle modification + AADs |
| 40886309 | ALONE-AF | 2025 | RCT | OAC discontinuation after successful ablation: non-inferior |

### Ablation in HFpEF (Emerging Evidence)

| PMID | Authors | Year | Design | Key Finding |
|------|---------|------|--------|-------------|
| 40243977 | Martens et al. | 2025 | CABANA post-hoc subanalysis | Ablation beneficial in HFpEF subgroup |
| 38656292 | Oraii et al. | 2024 | Meta-analysis | Ablation benefits in HFrEF > HFpEF, but both benefit |
| 39447815 | DeLuca et al. | 2025 | Multicenter comparative | Ablation vs AAD in HFpEF: ablation superior |
| 39023141 | Parwani et al. | 2024 | Trial protocol | CABA-HFPEF-DZHK27: ongoing dedicated RCT |

**Gap:** CASTLE-AF enrolled only HFrEF (EF ≤35%). CABANA's HFpEF subanalysis was
post-hoc and underpowered. CABA-HFPEF is ongoing but not yet reported. No TTE study
has emulated CASTLE-AF or CABANA in HFpEF patients using observational data.

## 4. Anticoagulation in CKD

### RCTs

| PMID | Trial/Authors | Year | Design | Key Finding |
|------|---------------|------|--------|-------------|
| 36335914 | RENAL-AF (Pokorney et al.) | 2022 | RCT, N=154 | Apixaban vs warfarin in hemodialysis: no significant difference in bleeding (underpowered) |
| 33753537 | De Vriese et al. | 2021 | RCT, N=132 | Rivaroxaban vs VKA in hemodialysis: rivaroxaban had more bleeding |

### Key Observational Studies

| PMID | Authors | Year | Design | Key Finding |
|------|---------|------|--------|-------------|
| 37839687 | Fu et al. | 2024 | US nationwide cohort | In advanced CKD: apixaban lower bleeding than rivaroxaban or warfarin |
| 33012172 | Yao et al. | 2020 | US cohort | OAC effectiveness varies by kidney function strata |
| 29954737 | Siontis et al. | 2018 | US observational | Apixaban in ESKD: lower bleeding than warfarin |
| 32640949 | Wetmore et al. | 2020 | Medicare cohort | DOACs vs warfarin in CKD+AF |

**Gap:** No head-to-head DOAC comparison in advanced CKD using TTE methodology.
Fu 2024 used propensity score matching but not formal TTE. Both RCTs (RENAL-AF,
De Vriese) were small, safety-focused, and compared DOACs to warfarin rather than
to each other. Apixaban vs rivaroxaban in advanced CKD is the most prescribing-
relevant unanswered question.

## 5. DOACs in Liver Disease

### Key Studies

| PMID | Authors | Year | Design | Key Finding |
|------|---------|------|--------|-------------|
| 38976880 | Simon et al. | 2024 | US nationwide cohort | Apixaban vs rivaroxaban vs warfarin in cirrhosis: apixaban lowest bleeding |
| 36762560 | Lawal et al. | 2023 | Nationwide cohort | DOACs vs warfarin in chronic liver disease: DOACs safer |
| 39053441 | Song et al. | 2024 | Real-world outcomes | Anticoag safety in cirrhosis+AF |

**Gap:** All cirrhosis studies are observational. Patients with Child-Pugh B/C
cirrhosis were excluded from all pivotal DOAC RCTs. No TTE study exists for
anticoagulation choice in AF with cirrhosis.

## 6. Left Atrial Appendage Closure

### Landmark RCTs

| PMID | Trial | Year | Design | Key Finding |
|------|-------|------|--------|-------------|
| 39555822 | OPTION | 2025 | RCT, N=1,600 | LAAC after ablation non-inferior to OAC |
| 41849741 | CLOSURE-AF | 2026 | RCT | LAAC vs medical therapy: LAAC was **NOT noninferior** (p=0.44 for noninferiority) |
| 32586585 | PRAGUE-17 | 2020 | RCT, N=402 | LAAC vs DOACs in high-risk: non-inferior |
| 33999547 | LAAOS III | 2021 | RCT, N=4,811 | Surgical LAA occlusion during cardiac surgery: reduced stroke |

### LAAC in Special Populations

| PMID | Authors | Year | Design | Key Finding |
|------|---------|------|--------|-------------|
| 40924421 | Dhar et al. | 2025 | Observational | LAAC vs anticoagulants in dialysis |
| 40231086 | Lu et al. | 2025 | **TTE** | LAAC vs DOACs for dementia risk: LAAC lower risk (HR 0.57) |

**Gap:** LAAC vs continued anticoagulation in patients with CKD stages 4-5
who have both high stroke and high bleeding risk is inadequately studied.
Dhar 2025 is the only direct comparison in dialysis patients, and it was
observational without formal TTE methodology.

| 38847907 | Truong et al. | 2025 | DOACs vs warfarin in AF+cancer | SEER-Medicare |
| 39394165 | Li et al. | 2024 | Suicide risk with different OACs | Nationwide (Taiwan) |

| 36852680 | Walkey et al. | 2023 | Anticoag after sepsis with new-onset AF | Kaiser Permanente |

**Key observation:** TTE methodology has been applied to AF anticoagulation
questions including head-to-head DOAC comparisons (PMID 36252244: apixaban vs
rivaroxaban in VHD; Mei 2021: dabigatran vs rivaroxaban), OAC initiation timing,
and resumption after events. However, TTE has NOT been applied to:
- Head-to-head DOAC comparisons in **CKD or liver disease**
- Rhythm vs rate control strategy
- Catheter ablation vs drugs in HF subgroups
- LAAC vs anticoagulation in high-bleeding-risk subgroups

These represent high-value TTE opportunities.
