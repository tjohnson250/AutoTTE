## 2. Landmark Randomized Controlled Trials

### 2.1 SGLT2 Inhibitor CVOTs (Placebo-Controlled)

| Trial | Drug | PMID | N | 3P-MACE HR (95% CI) | Key Findings |
|-------|------|------|---|---------------------|--------------|
| **CANVAS Program** | Canagliflozin | 28605608 | 10,142 | **0.86 (0.75–0.97)** | Superiority for MACE; increased amputation risk (HR 1.97); mean follow-up 188 weeks |
| **EMPA-REG OUTCOME** | Empagliflozin | 26378978 | 7,020 | **0.86 (0.74–0.99)** | 38% reduction in CV death; 35% reduction in HHF; no MI/stroke reduction individually |
| **DECLARE-TIMI 58** | Dapagliflozin | 30415602 | 17,160 | 0.93 (0.84–1.03) | Non-inferior but not superior for MACE; significant reduction in CV death/HHF (HR 0.83) |
| **CREDENCE** | Canagliflozin | 30990260 | 4,401 | — | Renal endpoint trial in CKD; 30% reduction in CV death/MI/stroke secondary endpoint |

**Class effect summary:** SGLT2 inhibitors as a class reduce MACE vs placebo, with empagliflozin and canagliflozin showing statistical superiority and dapagliflozin showing noninferiority. The CV benefit is driven primarily by reductions in CV death and HHF, with more modest effects on MI/stroke individually.

### 2.2 DPP-4 Inhibitor CVOTs (Placebo-Controlled)

| Trial | Drug | PMID | N | 3P-MACE HR (95% CI) | Key Findings |
|-------|------|------|---|---------------------|--------------|
| **SAVOR-TIMI 53** | Saxagliptin | 23992601 | 16,492 | 1.00 (0.89–1.12) | Noninferior; **increased HHF risk (HR 1.27)** |
| **TECOS** | Sitagliptin | 27437883 | 14,671 | ~1.01 (noninferior) | No increase in HHF; CV-neutral |
| **CAROLINA** | Linagliptin | 31536101 | 6,033 | — | Noninferior to glimepiride (active comparator); CV-neutral |

**Class effect summary:** Meta-analyses (PMID 41246652, PMID 34364771) confirm DPP-4 inhibitors are cardiovascularly neutral as a class (pooled HR ~0.99 for MACE). Saxagliptin carries a unique HHF signal; sitagliptin and linagliptin appear HF-neutral. This CV neutrality supports their use as an active comparator (proxy for placebo) in observational studies comparing SGLT2i CV effects.

### 2.3 Head-to-Head RCTs

| Trial | Comparison | PMID | N | Outcome | Key Finding |
|-------|-----------|------|---|---------|-------------|
| **CANTABILE** | Canagliflozin vs teneligliptin (DPP4i) | 34481910 | 162 | Metabolic risk factors | Canagliflozin superior for composite metabolic improvement (62% vs 31%) |
| **CANTABILE sub-analysis** | Canagliflozin vs teneligliptin | 39160513 | 145 | Energy intake, body weight | Canagliflozin: weight loss despite increased energy intake |

**Gap identified:** No head-to-head RCT of canagliflozin vs any DPP-4 inhibitor has used MACE as an outcome. CANTABILE was small (N=162) and focused on metabolic surrogates. This gap motivates observational TTE approaches.

## 4. Methodological Landscape

### 4.1 Target Trial Emulation in This Space

Studies explicitly using TTE framework for antidiabetic drug comparisons:

| Study | PMID | Comparison | Outcome | Year |
|-------|------|-----------|---------|------|
| Xie et al. | 37499675 | SGLT2i/GLP1RA/DPP4i/SU for MACE | 4-arm TTE with VA data | 2023 |
| Kosjerina et al. | 40201798 | GLP1RA/SGLT2i/DPP4i for MACE | 3-arm TTE in elderly, Danish data | 2025 |
| Saeed et al. | 40523289 | Semaglutide vs empagliflozin | Death/MI/stroke | 2025 |
| Shah et al. | 39985508 | SGLT2i for aortic stenosis | TTE in non-diabetes indication | 2025 |
| Multiple 2025-2026 | 41732861, 41574952, 41555834, 41790761 | SGLT2i/DPP4i/GLP1RA various | HHF, DKD mortality, pancreatitis, hypomagnesemia | 2025–2026 |

**Key observation:** TTE methodology is increasingly applied to antidiabetic drug comparisons, but **no published study has specifically emulated a canagliflozin-vs-DPP4i trial for 3P-MACE** as the primary question. Existing TTE studies either:
1. Compare SGLT2i as a class (not canagliflozin specifically) vs DPP4i
2. Use MACE as secondary rather than primary
3. Focus on non-MACE outcomes (HHF, renal, liver, etc.)

### 4.2 Active Comparator New-User Design

The DPP-4i-as-active-comparator approach is well-established in this literature (Patorno group: PMID 36745425, 38509341, 37677118; Al-Aly group: PMID 37499675). The rationale: DPP-4i are CV-neutral (from CVOTs), so they serve as a pharmacoepidemiologic placebo, avoiding confounding by indication inherent in untreated comparator groups.

The sulfonylurea-as-secondary-comparator approach is also supported (CAROLINA trial PMID 31536101 showed linagliptin noninferior to glimepiride; Xie et al. showed SU associated with highest MACE risk).

## 5. Safety Signals Relevant to Study Design

| Safety Issue | Evidence | Implications for Protocol |
|-------------|----------|--------------------------|
| **Canagliflozin amputation risk** | CANVAS HR 1.97 (PMID 28605608); OBSERVE-4D found no increase in routine care (PMID 29938883) | Must capture as safety endpoint; may differ from RCT in routine care population |
| **DKA risk with SGLT2i** | All CVOTs and CE studies show increased DKA (D'Andrea: HR elevated; EMPRISE: HR 1.78) | Capture as safety endpoint |
| **Saxagliptin HHF risk** | SAVOR-TIMI 53 HR 1.27 (PMID 23992601) | If DPP4i comparator includes saxagliptin users, may attenuate apparent SGLT2i HHF benefit |
| **Genital infections** | Consistent signal across all SGLT2i vs DPP4i studies | Important safety endpoint |

## 6. Summary of Evidence and Gaps

### What is well-established:
1. SGLT2i as a class reduce MACE vs placebo (RCTs) and vs DPP4i (observational)
2. DPP4i are CV-neutral vs placebo (RCTs) — valid active comparator
3. The active comparator new-user design is well-validated for this comparison
4. TTE methodology has been successfully applied to antidiabetic drug comparisons

### What remains uncertain:
1. **Canagliflozin-specific** MACE benefit vs DPP4i (most studies compare SGLT2i as class)
2. Whether canagliflozin's MACE benefit differs from other SGLT2i members
3. Whether the canagliflozin amputation signal persists in routine clinical practice with PCORnet data
4. Canagliflozin vs sulfonylurea for MACE (no direct TTE)
5. Whether a canagliflozin-specific TTE emulating CANVAS would replicate the trial's findings
6. Subgroup heterogeneity (by ASCVD history, CKD, HF, age) for canagliflozin specifically
