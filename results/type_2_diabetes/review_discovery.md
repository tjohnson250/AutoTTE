## Summary Verdict: REVISE

The literature scan is comprehensive and well-structured, covering the major CVOTs, observational studies, and methodological landscape with mostly accurate citations. However, I identified one **critical omission** — Filion et al. 2020 (BMJ, PMID 32967856), a multi-database study of 419,734 matched patients that provides **canagliflozin-specific 3P-MACE data vs DPP-4 inhibitors (HR 0.79, 95% CI 0.66–0.94)**. This directly contradicts the worker's claim that "no study has compared canagliflozin specifically vs DPP-4i for 3P-MACE." The evidence gap for Question 1 is narrower than presented, and the gap score is inflated. The question remains valid (no canagliflozin-specific TTE exists), but the framing must be corrected, and two additional missing studies should be incorporated.

## 2. Independent Search Results

### 2.1 Critical Finding: Filion et al. 2020 (BMJ) — Canagliflozin-Specific MACE vs DPP-4i

**Search:** `canagliflozin vs DPP-4 inhibitor MACE cardiovascular comparative effectiveness observational study`

**Found:** Filion KB, Lix LM, Yu OHY, et al. "Sodium glucose cotransporter 2 inhibitors and risk of major adverse cardiovascular events: multi-database retrospective cohort study." *BMJ.* 2020;370:m3342. **PMID 32967856.**

- **Design:** Multi-database retrospective cohort, prevalent new-user design with meta-analysis
- **N:** 209,867 SGLT2i users matched to 209,867 DPP4i users
- **Data Sources:** 7 Canadian provinces + UK (2013–2018)
- **Outcome:** 3-point MACE (MI, ischaemic stroke, CV death)
- **Overall SGLT2i vs DPP4i:** HR 0.76 (0.69–0.84)
- **Canagliflozin-specific:** HR **0.79 (0.66–0.94)** for MACE vs DPP4i
- **Dapagliflozin-specific:** HR 0.73 (0.63–0.85)
- **Empagliflozin-specific:** HR 0.77 (0.68–0.87)

**Impact:** This directly contradicts the worker's claim in the evidence gaps document that "no study has compared canagliflozin specifically vs DPP-4i for 3P-MACE." The study is published in BMJ (top-tier journal), has a very large sample, and provides canagliflozin-specific MACE data with a statistically significant result. The worker's three-pass search strategy should have identified this paper, which has been cited over 200 times.

### 2.2 LEGEND-T2DM (Patel et al. 2024, JACC)

**Search:** `LEGEND-T2DM SGLT2 inhibitor DPP4 MACE multinational`

**Found:** Patel A, et al. "Comparative Effectiveness of Second-Line Antihyperglycemic Agents for Cardiovascular Outcomes: A Multinational, Federated Analysis of LEGEND-T2DM." *JACC.* 2024;83(13):1290-1300.

- **Design:** Multinational federated analysis using 10 international data sources
- **N:** 5.2 million patient-years of follow-up; 25,982 3P-MACE events
- **Comparison:** SGLT2i vs DPP4i: HR 0.89 (0.79–1.00) for 3P-MACE
- **Note:** Class-level only, no canagliflozin-specific results reported in primary analysis

**Impact:** This is a major, high-profile comparative effectiveness study the worker did not cite. While class-level, it's one of the largest studies in this space and should be included.

### 2.3 Shin et al. 2025 (JAMA Internal Medicine) — Individual SGLT2i Comparison Using TTE

**Search:** `individual SGLT-2 inhibitors cardiovascular safety outcomes JAMA Internal Medicine`

**Found:** Shin H, Paik JM, Everett BM, et al. "Comparative Effectiveness of Individual Sodium-Glucose Cotransporter 2 Inhibitors." *JAMA Intern Med.* 2025. **PMID 39836397.**

- **Design:** Target trial emulation using 3 US claims databases
- **N:** 232,890 canagliflozin; 129,881 dapagliflozin; 295,043 empagliflozin
- **Canagliflozin vs empagliflozin:** MI/stroke HR 0.98 (0.91–1.05); HHF HR 1.00 (0.88–1.14)
- **Finding:** Individual SGLT2i have comparable CV effectiveness

**Impact:** The worker found this PMID in a targeted search (listed under Q4 amputation search) but did not describe it in the main tables or reference list. This study is highly relevant to the evidence gap about whether canagliflozin differs from other SGLT2i members (gap #2 in the Summary of Evidence section).

### 2.4 Kornelius et al. 2025 (Frontiers in Pharmacology) — Canagliflozin vs Selective SGLT2i

**Search:** `canagliflozin vs DPP-4 inhibitor MACE 2025 2026`

**Found:** Kornelius E, Lo SC, Yang YS, et al. "Comparative cardiovascular risks of canagliflozin and selective SGLT2 inhibitors in type 2 diabetes." *Front Pharmacol.* 2025.

- **Design:** Retrospective cohort, TriNetX data, N=24,078
- **Comparison:** Canagliflozin vs empagliflozin/dapagliflozin/ertugliflozin
- **MACE:** HR 1.23 (1.14–1.33) — canagliflozin had **higher** MACE risk than selective SGLT2i

**Impact:** Not about canagliflozin vs DPP4i, but highly relevant to the evidence landscape. This finding suggests canagliflozin may have a *less favorable* CV profile than other SGLT2i, which is important context for interpreting the proposed study.

## 3. Stress-Testing Key Claims

### Claim 1: "No study has compared canagliflozin specifically vs DPP-4i for 3P-MACE"

**Worker's stress test:** PubMed search (89 hits), PubMed search (78 hits), WebSearch. Worker concluded "validated."

**My independent verification:** **CLAIM IS FALSE.**

Filion et al. 2020 (BMJ, PMID 32967856) provides canagliflozin-specific 3P-MACE data vs DPP-4i: HR 0.79 (0.66–0.94). This is a 419,734-patient multi-database study from BMJ. It is not a TTE, but the worker's claim was about *any* canagliflozin vs DPP4i MACE comparison, not specifically about TTE.

The narrower claim — "no published study has specifically emulated a canagliflozin-vs-DPP4i trial for 3P-MACE as the primary question" — appears to still hold based on my searches. No TTE specifically designs around canagliflozin vs DPP4i for 3P-MACE as the primary estimand.

**Verdict:** The evidence gap framing must be corrected. The gap is about TTE-specific methodology and canagliflozin-as-primary-focus, not about a total absence of canagliflozin-specific MACE data vs DPP4i.

### Claim 2: "No canagliflozin-specific vs SU TTE for 3P-MACE"

**My searches:** `canagliflozin sulfonylurea cardiovascular MACE comparative effectiveness 2024 2025 2026`

**Finding:** No canagliflozin-specific vs SU study for 3P-MACE found. Xie 2023 has class-level SGLT2i vs SU. GRADE (PMID 38344820) includes glimepiride but not in the context of canagliflozin.

**Verdict:** **Claim validated.**

### Claim 3: "No study has specifically emulated a canagliflozin-vs-DPP4i trial for 3P-MACE" (TTE-specific)

**My searches:** `"target trial emulation" canagliflozin cardiovascular MACE`; `"target trial emulation" SGLT2 inhibitor DPP-4 inhibitor MACE systematic review 2025 2026`

**Finding:** Existing TTE studies (Xie 2023, Kosjerina 2025, Siriyotha 2023) compare SGLT2i as a class vs DPP4i. None isolate canagliflozin as the specific intervention in a TTE framework for 3P-MACE.

**Self-consistency check:** Verified that none of the worker's cited papers perform this specific canagliflozin-only TTE. Xie 2023 uses SGLT2i class. Kosjerina 2025 uses SGLT2i class. D'Andrea 2023 is PS-matched, not TTE. EMPRISE is empagliflozin-specific.

**Verdict:** **Claim validated** (narrowly).

### Claim 4: PMID 41204979 classified as "New-user CE" — self-consistency issue

The worker classifies Wu et al. 2026 (PMID 41204979) as a "New-user CE" study in Section 3.1 of the literature scan. However, the published paper explicitly describes itself as a "population-based, target trial emulation cohort study." This means the worker's TTE inventory in Section 4.1 is incomplete — it misses an additional TTE study it already cited.

**Verdict:** Minor misclassification. Should be reclassified as TTE in Section 4.1.
